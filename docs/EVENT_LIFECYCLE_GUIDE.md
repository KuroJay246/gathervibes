# Event Lifecycle Guide

Use event status to show where the event is in its real workflow. Do not use raw status codes in organizer communication.

## Draft

- Meaning: the event idea exists, but key details are still being captured.
- Do next: confirm the name, date, venue, time, and rough capacity.

## Planning

- Meaning: the event is being organized and the team is building the budget, registrations, partners, tasks, and readiness.
- Do next: add planning tasks, partner records, and first registrations.

## Registration Open

- Meaning: guests can register and payments should be monitored closely.
- Do next: watch `Payments`, assign tickets as needed, and keep communications ready.

## Registration Closed

- Meaning: registration intake is paused while final preparation continues.
- Do next: confirm guest counts, complete tickets, and close readiness gaps.

## Ready for Event

- Meaning: the core event-day requirements are in place.
- Do next: confirm the event-day timeline, urgent contacts, check-in process, and supplies.

## In Progress

- Meaning: the event is currently running.
- Do next: use `Overview`, `Check-In`, `Tickets`, `Operations`, and the timeline for live event work.

## Completed

- Meaning: the event has finished and the organizer is reviewing results and closeout work.
- Do next: review attendance, payments, unpaid commitments, sponsor support, baker payments, and final follow-up.

## Archived

- Meaning: the event is finished and retained for history.
- Do next: keep it for reference only.

## Cancelled

- Meaning: the event will not run, but the record should stay visible for history.
- Do next: stop normal event actions and document any closeout notes that matter.

## Legacy labels

Older records may still contain `Upcoming` or `Active`. In the current product they map to `Planning` and `In Progress`.
